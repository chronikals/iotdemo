					<p class="lead">Colors used for ABB digital products are based on the primary and secondary colors defined in the guidelines for ABB’s

					<!--Look and feel-Typography -->
					<p style="height:100px;" />
					<h2 id="lookandfeel-typography">Typography</h2><span class="label label-success">New</span>
					<p class="lead">Neue Helvetica eText is the corporate typeface designed especially for clear display and legibility on the full range
						of devices such as smartphones, tablets, eReaders and the Web. It provides improved performance and readability on small
						screens and in low resolution environments. Use the Neue Helvetica eText font primarily in its light and medium styles.
						Bold should only be used in exceptional cases when extra emphasis is required. Other versions of this typeface, such
						as condensed, expanded or outline, shall not be used. The typeface has been licensed by GF-CC (Corporate Communications)
					for ABB software and web applications. Use Arial as fallback font when something prevents use of Neue Helvetica eText.</p>
					<img src="images/lookandfeel-typography.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
					
					<!--Look and feel-Icons -->
					<p style="height:100px;">
					<h2 id="lookandfeel-icons">Icons</h2></p>
					<p class="lead">Icons are signs that represents an object, a concept, a service or an action. The goal of icons is to provide information
						quickly and to enable the user to easily and intuitively navigate the system. To secure a high value of recognition,
						consistent qualitative brand appearance and optimized navigation/orientation, all icons should be based on the same
						design rules. Icons from ABB are reduced to the minimum. No unnecessary details and decoration is used. The ABB brand
						appearance is clear and precise. In terms of a specific design language this means that e.g. no rounded frames must
					be used.</h4>
				</p>
				<div class="row">
					<div class="col-xs-4">
						<img src="images/lookandfeel-icons1.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
						<p class="lead">
						<h3>Primary icon style</h3></p>
						<p>
							<h4>The primary icon style is filled objects which assures a high readibility also in small sizes.
							Icons will be monochrome, but context related colour highlights (notifications/badges) are permitted. </h4></p>
							
					</div>
					<div class="col-xs-4">
						<img src="images/lookandfeel-icons2.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
						<p class="lead">
						<h3>Secondary icon style</h3></p>
						<p>
							<h4>In some cases the primary icon style can’t be applied. In order to get a more detailed illustration the secondary icon style with two different line strokes can be used. The thicker line frames the icon and the thinner line provides detail inside the frame.
							Primary and secondary icons can’t be mixed in the same context. (Same tool bar, prosess graphic etc).</h4></p>
							
					</div>
					<div class="col-xs-4">
						<img src="images/lookandfeel-icons3.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
						<p class="lead">
						<h3>Rules</h3></p>
						<p>
							<h4>Keep it simple. Clear and sharp. 
								Do not combine filled shapes and lines
								The size must be consistent
								Multiple colors (see exception) are not allowed
							Perspectives shall not be used.</p>

						<img src="images/layout-intro.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
						<p class="lead"><h3>Layout means the arrangement of visual elements on a page/screen</h3></p>
						<p>Layout establishes a relationship
							between various elements on a
							page. ABB has a variety of systems
							and data-heavy interfaces.
							Layout principles help create
							an underlying guideline that will
							ensure an organized and user
						friendly system that is efficient.</p>	
						
						<p style="height:100px;"/>
						<h2 id="layout-basics">The basics</h2>
						<div class="col-xs-5">
							<p class="lead"><h3>Hierarchy</h3></p>
							<p>A visual hierarchy influences the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees. Size and colour can help to highlight objects.</p>
							<img src="images/layout-basics1.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%; margin-top:27px;">
						</div>
						<div class="col-xs-5 col-xs-offset-2">
							<p class="lead"><h3>Balance</h3></p>
							<p>Placing too much on one side can make your product feel uneven and heavy which is uncomfortable for the eyes. A balanced layout ensures everything proportionally works together and is weighted the same.</p>
							<img src="images/layout-basics2.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
						</div>
						
						<p style="height:100px;"/>
						<h2 id="layout-principles">Principles</h2>
						<div class="col-xs-5">
							<p class="lead"><h3>Intiutive</h3></p>
							<p>Layouts should follow a regular reading flow from top to bottom, left to right. Keep it familiar to the user.</p>
							<img src="images/layout-principles1.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%;">
						</div>
						<div class="col-xs-5 col-xs-offset-2">
							<p class="lead"><h3>Focus</h3></p>
							<p>Don’t overcomplicate it for the user, hide uneccesary infomation, and show only what is relevant. Too many options, makes for a more chaotic experience.</p>
							<img src="images/layout-principles2.PNG" alt="ABB black bar" style="width:100%; margin-top:20px;">
